What to do when you can't do anything
Or why it's so hard for us to concentrate, especially when it's really needed

Why Concentration is So Difficult

Multitasking

It's especially hard to concentrate when there are many tasks and all of them are important. Where is the legendary multitasking when you (and all the hiring managers in the world) need it the most? The thing is, it simply doesn't exist. Researchers found out that the brain struggles to focus even on two tasks at the same time. And when several important tasks come into focus, the body panics and releases cortisol and adrenaline — the "stress hormones". Because of this, we work carelessly: making mistakes and getting tired quickly.

Dopamine

It's clear with stress hormones, but there's more interesting stuff ahead. Our inability to focus also involves dopamine, which is completely opposite to stress. This substance participates in the brain's reward system. What's it doing here? We often get distracted from an important task by something more "pleasant" for the brain. For example, checking likes on social networks. At this time, dopamine is released — and we feel pleasure. It turns out to be a vicious circle: the more you get distracted, the more pleasure you get.

What Lowers Concentration?

Multitasking

How can concentration decrease because of... concentration? Any stressful situation (and the rapidly changing world in general) makes our brain constantly "scan" the environment for danger. For example, reading news instead of working. But it's impossible to be focused all the time. Concentration is like a muscle, and it can fail if not allowed to rest.

Food

"Fast carbs" — sugar, white bread, sweets — deliver energy to the brain lightning-fast and help it work better. But this energy disappears just as quickly as it arrives. And we end up on "carbohydrate swings": as soon as the effect of "fast carbs" ends, we feel tired and sleepy. And we want more fast carbs.

Gadgets

Yes, it's that very situation when the laptop screen shows code, the headphones play a podcast, and a video with funny ducks is playing in the background on the computer. As a result, the brain tries to switch from one task to another — and just can't. And to return to a state of full concentration, a person needs an average of 20 minutes. So, the more time we spend on gadgets, the more time we then need to get back to work.

How to Concentrate Better Than a Goldfish (i.e., Longer Than Three Seconds)

5 tips (not necessarily easy) for improving concentration

  • Imagine the Sky and Clouds

    Or leaves in a stream. It's about easy meditation that helps to calm down. Often, it's the anxious thoughts about difficult tasks that prevent these tasks from being accomplished. At such moments, you can close your eyes and imagine that the clear sky is you, and the clouds are your anxious thoughts. No matter how many there are — the clear sky will always be there, behind the clouds, and won't go anywhere. This exercise will help to return to the present moment, rather than worrying about potential problems from the future.

  • Play music

    But not any, and not your favorite. Your favorite might be associated with strong positive emotions, which also distract a lot. Suitable is quiet, calm, moderately rhythmic music. You can even try a playlist with "white noise" or neuro music.

  • Go for a Walk

    Or even run. It's better to find a park or forest: one experiment showed that contemplating nature increases concentration, while after viewing urban landscapes it decreases. You can combine a walk with physical exercises or running — this way, more oxygen will enter the brain and it will work better. But even a simple twenty-minute walk is a great option.

  • Eat Well

    We're not your grandmother, but it's really important. Choose foods that prevent sharp spikes in blood sugar. For example, those high in fiber: vegetables, berries, legumes (chickpeas, lentils, beans), brown rice, whole grain wheat bread.

  • Read

    Real paper books. It truly helps with concentration: to read a post on social networks, you need a few minutes or even seconds. But an interesting book can engage you for several hours and greatly trains directed attention.
